# Innovating Robotics: Highlights from Robot Builders Night – March 10, 2026

The Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) once again gathered for an inspiring and interactive Virtual Robot Builders Night. This month’s session showcased emerging projects, spirited discussions on technical challenges, and announcements about upcoming robotics competitions, reinforcing the community’s commitment to creativity and engineering excellence.

---

## A Look at Upcoming Competitions

The event kicked off with exciting updates on several robot competitions designed to challenge and inspire the community:

- **RoboRama**: Set for the weekend of May 24th, RoboRama will host a variety of competitions including line following, barrel racing, sumo, and more. Participants have been urged to fine-tune their robots to tackle these diverse challenges.
  
- **Line Following Parade**: In a bid to blend creativity with technology, a new event at Dallas College Eastfield was proposed. This parade features floats designed to autonomously follow designated lines. Robotics enthusiasts are encouraged to experiment with electronic control systems to breathe life into their innovative float designs.

---

## Project Updates: From Design to Execution

The meeting showcased several ongoing projects with a mix of challenges and breakthroughs that epitomize the vibrant spirit of robotics innovation:

- **Robbie the Robot**: Led by Mark and his team, progress is being made on streamlining Robbie’s power supply. The initiative aims to replace multiple power sources with consolidated 12V and 5V systems, simplifying internal wiring. Additionally, a CAD model and a 3D-printed neck mechanism prototype have been developed to enhance Robbie's range of motion.
  
- **Sesame Robot**: Tom Crawford brought forward updates on the Sesame Robot project. Facing challenges with servo fitting and power supply adjustments, Tom is re-evaluating battery and power management solutions to overcome these hurdles.
  
- **Tool Software Developments**: In a surprising twist, Doug discussed LightBurn’s foray into milling software with their new tool, Mill Mage. This software is poised to deliver a user-friendly CNC routing experience, similar to LightBurn’s well-known laser software, potentially bridging the gap between different fabrication techniques.

---

## Tackling Technical Challenges

Innovation often stems from overcoming obstacles. This meeting delved into hands-on problem-solving with discussions on:

- **2/3D Printing & Mold Making**: Members exchanged ideas on fabricating complex components such as car air intake manifolds using 3D-printed molds and silicone castings. The dialogue included insights into utilizing flexible materials and evaluating rubber hose alternatives for effective mold production.
  
- **Electrical Engineering Queries**: Mark Dombrowski sparked a technical discussion around preventing back feed in power supply circuits. The consensus highlighted the use of ideal diodes and MOSFETs as robust solutions to ensure safe and efficient power management, marking a crucial learning moment for both emerging and seasoned engineers.

---

## Reflections and Future Directions

The meeting’s energy was palpable as members explored diverse projects and challenges. Key takeaways include:

- A shared enthusiasm for participating in external robotics events not only as a competitive platform but also as a means to showcase innovative projects and promote collaborative learning within the DPRG community.
- Valuable insights into problem-solving techniques across mechanical design, power management, and software development, underscoring the interdisciplinary nature of modern robotics.

As DPRG gears up for future competitions and project enhancements, the collaborative spirit maintains its role as the bedrock for continual innovation in robotics.
