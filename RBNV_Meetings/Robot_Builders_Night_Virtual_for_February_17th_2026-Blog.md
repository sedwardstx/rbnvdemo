# Innovating Together: Highlights from Robot Builders Night Virtual – February 17, 2026

On February 17, 2026, the Dallas Personal Robotics Group convened for another dynamic session of Robot Builders Night Virtual (RBNV). This month’s meeting showcased a vibrant exchange of ideas, innovative project demonstrations, and technical workshops that spanned mechanical engineering, electronics, and advanced control systems. From custom-built robot chassis to enhanced laser cutter controls and AI-assisted coding, members are clearly pushing the boundaries of robotics design and integration.

## Breaking New Ground with Modular Robotics

### Mark Dombrowski: Redefining the Robot Chassis

Mark Dombrowski captivated the audience with his latest robot chassis design—a prototype that merges creative mechanical engineering with pragmatic electronic control. Key highlights of his presentation included:

- **Innovative Wheel Mechanism:** Utilizing 3D printed bearings and motor-integrated wheels, Mark's design leverages N20 motors paired with airsoft BBs as bearings. This novel approach not only reduces friction but also enhances cost-effectiveness.
- **Magnetic Angle Sensing:** To achieve independent wheel motion detection, a magnetic angle sensor has been integrated into the system. This precision sensing is crucial for accurate robotic navigation and control.
- **Custom Communication Protocol:** Mark outlined progress on his PCB designs that support a custom UART-based communication bus. This bus connects various modules, leveraging Raspberry Pi Picos for a robust, distributed robotic network. Such modularity can streamline integration and potentially accelerate robotics development by allowing for easy sharing of hardware components.

The focus on modular, shareable components—touted as a cornerstone for future robotics development—highlights how community collaboration can catalyze rapid innovation.

### Doug Paradis: Elevating Laser Cutter Performance

Doug Paradis shared compelling upgrades to his Monport Reno 65 Pro laser cutter, reflecting an intersection of robotics and precision machinery. His enhancements are particularly significant for makers and professionals looking to achieve cleaner cuts and greater material fidelity. Notable improvements include:

- **Air Assist System Redesign:** Doug replaced the default aquarium pump with a more reliable system that uses an independent relay and solenoid for compressor-controlled air pressure. This adjustment minimizes material burning—a common issue during laser cutting—while boosting operational efficiency.
- **Modular Controls for Versatility:** By advocating for modularity in air assist controls, Doug’s approach allows for quick switching between different air pressure settings. This flexibility can optimize performance across various materials, from delicate plastics to robust metals.

For more details, check out the [Monport Reno65 Pro](https://monportlaser.com/products/reno65-pro) and related resources on Amazon and the [CW-5200 Chiller](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DYV12LRZ?th=1).

## Expanding Mechanical Horizons with AI Integration

### Mike Williamson: Building Heavy-Duty Platforms

Mike Williamson’s presentation was dedicated to platform development—focusing on creating robust structures capable of supporting significant weight. His project incorporated:

- **Advanced Rotation Mechanics:** Integrating casters and dedicated drive wheels, Mike's platform is designed to handle the rigors of heavy-duty operation.
- **Wi-Fi and AI-Assisted Control Systems:** Utilizing microcontrollers enhanced with AI-assisted programming, his control system ensures precise maneuverability and adaptability. The use of Wi-Fi for remote control adds another layer of flexibility, facilitating real-time adjustments during operation.

Mike’s work provides valuable insights into how traditional mechanical setups can be enhanced using modern connectivity and AI tools.

### Harold Pulcher: Optimizing Code with AI Tools

In today’s digital realm, coding efficiency is paramount. Harold Pulcher impressed the meeting with his strategic insights on using AI tools like GitHub’s Co-pilot:

- **Smart Code Assistance:** Leveraging AI for coding not only expedites the development process but also helps in managing token usage effectively—a critical factor as projects scale.
- **Workflow Management:** Harold’s tips on integrating AI-driven coding assistance into regular workflows resonated with both newcomers and seasoned developers, underlining the importance of harnessing advanced tools to boost productivity.
