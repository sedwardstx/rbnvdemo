# Charting the Future of Robotics: RTK GPS Innovations & Advanced Learning Resources

The June 16th, 2026 Robot Builders Night Virtual session brought together robotics enthusiasts and professionals to explore the latest in navigational precision and robotics education. This week’s meeting spotlighted advancements in Real Time Kinematic (RTK) GPS systems—a technology that is rapidly transforming the way robots navigate—and highlighted an educational resource from ETH Zurich for those eager to deepen their robotics expertise.

## Navigational Precision with RTK GPS Systems

One of the standout topics discussed during the meeting was the application of RTK GPS systems in robotics. RTK technology leverages satellite data and correction signals to provide location accuracy at the centimeter level—essential for robotics applications that demand high precision.

### In-Depth Look at the lc29h_da RTK GPS Driver

Paul Bouchier shared insights into his work on a driver for the lc29h_da RTK GPS system. Key discussion points included:
- **Project Repository:** Paul’s GitHub repository for the RTK GPS driver is an invaluable resource for those looking to implement or adapt this technology in their projects. You can explore the repository here: [lc29h_da RTK GPS Driver GitHub](https://github.com/PaulBouchier/lc29h_da_rtk_gps_driver).
- **Cost Considerations:** With a unit price of $73 for both the rover and the base station, the discussion emphasized the importance of assessing project requirements. For many applications, especially where pinpoint accuracy isn’t mandatory, a base station may not be necessary—allowing teams to adopt cost-effective navigation solutions without compromising on performance.

These insights underscore the potential of RTK technology in enhancing the reliability and precision of autonomous robotics systems, paving the way for innovations in fields ranging from industrial automation to precision agriculture.
