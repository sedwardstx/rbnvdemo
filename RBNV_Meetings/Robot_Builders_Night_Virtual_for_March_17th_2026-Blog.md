# Charting New Frontiers: A Recap of the March 17th, 2026 Robot Builders Night Virtual Meeting

The Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) is always at the forefront of innovation, and their latest Robot Builders Night Virtual meeting on March 17, 2026, was no exception. This session provided an exciting mix of competition announcements, project updates, and discussions on emerging technologies—from ultrasonic sensors and laser diodes to animatronic creations and centimeter-level GNSS positioning. Let’s dive into the key takeaways from the meeting.

## Project Updates from the Field

### Doug Paradis' Zamboni-Inspired Innovation

Doug Paradis stunned the audience with updates on his creatively re-imagined robot. His project, inspired by a Zamboni, was modified to participate in a line-following parade. Key highlights include:

- **Design Enhancements**: Incorporating a Zamboni-style add-on with integrated ultrasonic sensors for precise distance calculations.
- **Troubleshooting Challenges**: Addressing issues with an Australian ultrasonic sensor product, a reminder that iterative troubleshooting is at the heart of robotics innovation.

Doug’s approach exemplifies the constant blend of creativity and technical precision required to push the boundaries of robotic design.

### Mark R’s Quadruped and Animatronic Explorations

Mark R. captivated attendees with a dual showcase:

- **Quadruped Robot Project**: This versatile project continues to evolve with plans for integrating various sensors and modules, aimed at improving adaptability across diverse tasks.
- **Animatronic Eye Class**: In an inspiring twist, Mark also discussed his plans to teach a class focused on creating lifelike animatronic eyes. Using 3D printing and electronics, the project highlights how creative applications of technology can bridge art and robotics.

For further insights on animatronic projects, you can explore related initiatives by [Will Cogley](https://willcogley.notion.site/).

## Innovative Ideas and Emerging Technologies

### Laser Diodes for Engraving and Cutting

A lively discussion unfolded around the use of laser diodes—not just for standard applications but also for cutting and engraving projects. This exploration of beam combinations underlines a broader trend: leveraging existing technology in new, unexpected ways to address creative challenges.

### Introducing TinkerNav

Pat Caron introduced the TinkerNav, a breakthrough tool offering centimeter-level GNSS RTK positioning at a remarkably affordable price point. This product is poised to democratize access to precise robotics navigation, opening new avenues for both hobbyists and professionals alike. For an in-depth look, check out the [TinkerNav GPS Article](https://www.hackster.io/news/the-sub-100-tinkernav-puts-centimeter-level-gnss-rtk-positioning-in-the-palm-of-your-hand-60ce30dce15b).

## Conclusions and Future Directions

The March 17th session highlighted several enduring themes in robotics:
