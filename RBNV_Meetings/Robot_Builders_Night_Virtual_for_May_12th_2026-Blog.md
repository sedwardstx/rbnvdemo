## A Closer Look at the OpenMV AE3 Camera

Pat Caron kicked off the meeting by introducing the OpenMV AE3 camera—a compact but powerful vision engine ideal for robotics applications. Key takeaways include: 

- **Ease of Integration**: Designed to simplify the process for hobbyists and professionals, the OpenMV AE3 is well-suited for rapid prototyping in computer vision tasks.
- **Performance and Versatility**: With its advanced processing capabilities, the camera can support applications ranging from object tracking to real-time image processing.

For more details on the camera’s features and potential applications, check out the product page on [OpenMV AE3 Camera](https://openmv.io/collections/cameras/products/openmv-ae3).

## Deep Dive into TOF Range Sensor Technology

Paul Bouchier directed attention to the latest developments in Time of Flight (TOF) sensor technology with a focus on STMicroelectronics' 2.3k TOF range sensor. Highlights from his presentation include:

- **Advanced Sensing Capabilities**: TOF sensors provide high-precision distance measurements, which are vital for navigation and object detection in autonomous systems.
- **Cost and Accessibility**: Priced around $60-70, these sensors offer a balanced blend of performance and affordability, making them an attractive option for both academic research and commercial development.
- **Resource Availability**: Additional details and purchasing options can be found on [STMicroelectronics TOF Modules](https://www.mouser.com/new/stmicroelectronics/stmicroelectronics-vl53lp-tof-modules/?srsltid=AfmBOorTtypKC5GdnpewCccEdz2qZeSI7XKHAUizh9B_tt6hW42J8le8).
