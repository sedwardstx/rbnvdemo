# Robot Builders Night Virtual for April 7th, 2026

## Introduction
This session of the Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) featured extensive discussions and demonstrations related to robotics projects, competition preparations, and innovative applications of robotics tools and technology. The meeting covered various robot builds, challenges encountered, recent trips, and upcoming events of interest to the robotics community.

## Key Discussion Topics

### Robotics Presentations and Demonstrations
- **Barrel Racing Robot Update**: Pat Caron mentioned the upcoming Robo Rama competition, highlighting events like barrel racing. Ray demonstrated his barrel racing robot, discussing the challenges of sensor integration, odometry, and compensating for sensor lag during turns.
- **Arduino Uno Q**: Karim Virani introduced his latest project involving the Arduino Uno Q, aiming to reduce the footprint of his robot controller by transitioning from a Raspberry Pi. He elaborated on Wi-Fi connectivity issues and software bugs, explaining the transition to OpenCV for color detection and vision processing.
- **Mechanum Wheel Platform**: Ray showcased a mechanum wheel platform equipped with Max Cam, IMU, and distance sensors for orientation and navigation. The platform seeks to autonomously adjust its heading and center in relation to goals detected by sensors.

### Personal Experiences and Travels
- **Sailing Adventure**: Paul Bouchier shared his extensive three-month sailing trip starting in Thailand and ending in Egypt's Suez Canal, sharing insights about the unique challenges faced such as fixing diesel engine troubles at sea and witnessing extraordinary marine life and geography.

### Technology In-Depth
- **GitHub Pages for Documentation**: Paul Bouchier discussed the use of GitHub pages for documenting and sharing information about Linorobot2, explaining how markdown files can be converted into website pages, and briefly describing system architectures and configuration possibilities for both physical and simulated robots.
- **Remote Configuration with Docker**: Paul explained the concept of running ROS tools on cloud nodes using Docker, overcoming typical communication cost challenges by employing a virtual monitor setup in the cloud.

### Upcoming Events and Community Engagement
- Participants were reminded of the upcoming Robo Rama club competition to be held on May 23rd at the Dallas Maker Space.
- Doug highlighted the challenges in preparing for the club’s line-following parade on April 11th, demonstrating how a line-following parade robot was prepared amidst technical trials and integration of unique features such as an animated float.

## Conclusions and Insights
- Several robots showcased promise, yet more work is needed to fine-tune sensor integration and enhance performance accuracy.
- Arduino Uno Q offers an effective option for reduced controller footprint but presents technical bugs that need addressing.
- The integration of GitHub pages proves to be a beneficial tool for organizing and sharing robotics documentation.
- Community engagement through competitions continues to foster collaboration and innovation.
