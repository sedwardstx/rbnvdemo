# Exploring Robotics Innovation: Highlights from Robot Builders Night Virtual, March 24th, 2026

The March 24th session of the Robot Builders Night Virtual was a vibrant exchange of ideas, projects, and emerging technologies in robotics. From announcing competitive events to showcasing cutting-edge hardware, members of the Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) demonstrated that the future of robotics is both collaborative and rapidly evolving. In this blog post, we dive into the key discussion points and innovative projects that marked the evening.

---

## Robotics Competitions and Community Events

The meeting kicked off with an exciting announcement for the upcoming Roboama competitions scheduled for May 2026 at the Dallas Maker Space. Participants will face challenges such as quick trip line following, advanced barrel racing, six can, four square, and sumo events. These competitions promise to foster creativity and practical benchwork in robotics design and control.

Additionally, the community highlighted the March monthly meeting featuring a presentation by the creator of an inexpensive differential drive robot. This robot, equipped with LIDAR and turnkey ROS 2 software, underscores the commitment to making cutting-edge robotics accessible. The fusion of cost-effective design with advanced navigation and control systems is a clear indicator of the innovative spirit driving DPRG forward.

---

## Project Showcase: The Mini Robot Parade Float

In one of the session’s standout presentations, Mark R. introduced his mini robot parade float project. This creative initiative integrated modeled chains, gears, and a dynamic line-following system. Through iterative design tweaks and printed prototypes, Mark’s project sparked lively discussions on the balance between scale and performance in robotics design. His work not only exemplifies practical engineering but also illustrates the playful intersection where art meets robotics.

---

## Introducing the Arduino Ventuno Platform

A major highlight of the meeting was Doug Paradis’s introduction of the Arduino Ventuno platform. Designed specifically for advanced AI and robotics applications, the Ventuno boasts a dual-processor architecture that supports both real-time operations and neural processing units. This new board was compared to established platforms such as the [NVIDIA Jetson Nano](https://developer.nvidia.com/embedded/jetson-nano-developer-kit) and the [Raspberry Pi](https://www.raspberrypi.com/), renowned for their AI capabilities.

However, discussions raised valid concerns regarding the platform’s pricing and long-term ecosystem adoption. As robotics enthusiasts continue to push the envelope of what is possible, the balance between advanced features and cost-effective implementation remains a pivotal topic.

---

## GPS & RTK Positioning Technology: Precision Meets Affordability

Another significant topic was the advancement of GPS and RTK technologies in robotics. Affordable GNSS RTK units, such as those highlighted in the [Tinkernav article on Hackster.io](https://www.hackster.io/news/the-sub-100-tinkernav-puts-centimeter-level-gnss-rtk-positioning-in-the-palm-of-your-hand-60ce30dce15b), are now achieving centimeter-level accuracy. These innovations have the potential to transform outdoor robotics competitions by providing precise positioning data.

Participants, including contributors like Ponder SomeMore, weighed in on connectivity features and data acquisition methods, stressing how crucial it is to integrate these systems seamlessly with both proprietary and open-source platforms.

---

## Conclusion and Future Outlook

The March 24th Robot Builders Night Virtual meeting was a testament to the DPRG community’s drive to explore innovative technologies while keeping practical challenges in mind. Whether it was through competitive events designed to inspire real-time problem solving, creative project showcases like the mini robot parade float, or deep dives into next-generation hardware and positioning systems, the session demonstrated that collaboration and shared expertise will continue to propel robotics into new frontiers.

As robotics evolves to meet both emerging challenges and innovative opportunities, the interplay between advanced tech and cost-effective solutions remains at the forefront. Keep an eye on our upcoming posts for more insights and previews of what the future holds for robotics enthusiasts.
