# DPRG Monthly Mtg for April 25th, 2026

## [DPRG - Monthly Meeting - Apr 25, 2026 - YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XWBiDGHmxjU)

## Club Announcements & Upcoming Events

- **Roboama:** The next Roboama competition is scheduled for May 23rd. Featured events will include QuickRip, barrel racing, and sumo. The advanced line-following course will be particularly challenging this year, featuring a segmented S-curve, a 90-degree turn, and a "gap" element that breaks the rule of turning into the loss curve.
- **Giveaway:** A huge thank you to Sensory Robotics for generously donating robotic mower parts for club members. Available items include motors (both with and without single-channel encoders), wheels, brushless DC blade motors, and LCD panels.

## Guest Presentation: Sensory Robotics

- Ross and David Melbourne, along with their robot engineers Isaiah and Ashu, discussed the company's pivot from "stay with crew" robotic mowers to physical AI and humanoid robotics.
- They shared insights on how Vision Language Action (VLA) models and "zero-shot" learning are revolutionizing the robotics industry. These models extend large language models by adding action control, enabling robots to learn complex tasks—like folding cloth or sorting specific objects—from simple text prompts and very few training episodes.
- **Live Demonstration:** The team showcased their Uni Tree GoTo Robot, a heavy, 30-pound wheeled quadruped capable of reaching up to 17 mph. During the live demo, it successfully used its gyroscope to perform a handstand and climbed onto a box. The team noted that this robot is currently an R&D tool and requires careful setup and respect due to safety concerns and its lack of auto-calibration.

## Member Show and Tell

- **Animatronic Head:** Jason showed his animatronic robot prototype built to bring video game and movie technology to real life was showcased. Powered by an Arduino and Tango 3D animation software, it currently uses 14 servos, with plans to eventually expand to a full-sized robot on a sliding mechanism controlled by a Raspberry Pi.
- **Barrel Racing ROS Robot:** Mike presented his ROS-based robot equipped with a Raspberry Pi, Lidar, and a camera. It runs scripts to detect and navigate around cans spaced 6 to 8 feet apart on the Roboama competition course.
- **Mini Sumo Robot:** Mike also showed off his 495-gram Mini Sumo robot, optimized to sit just under the 500-gram competition limit. Built for traction with silicon wheels and an RP2040 micro board, it utilizes a front-facing time-of-flight sensor to detect targets, which also doubles as a 5-second "go switch" before a match.
