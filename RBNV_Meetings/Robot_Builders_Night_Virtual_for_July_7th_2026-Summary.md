## Presenters

- **Three-Phase Brushless Motor Platform:** Ray C. showcased his platform built with hoverboard motors. He successfully tested it at 12 volts, achieving a tracking speed of about 3 mph in autonomous mode. Gold star ⭐
  
  
- **Robbie Robot Head Rebuild:** Mike W. and Mark D. shared updates on the ROI robot head rebuild. To improve reliability and power, they upgraded to a Nema 17 stepper motor, which increases the holding force to five pounds. They also implemented an adjustment fix for the gear meshing and demonstrated a custom Arduino shield for stall detection.  Black star ★
  
  
- **RPI MediaPipe Face Tracking:** Paul B. gave an impressive demonstration of a new face-tracking program using Google's MediaPipe on a Raspberry Pi 4. The new setup is incredibly fast and handles rotation and angles much better than previous iterations. The team's next step is to integrate this software with Mike W.'s hardware controls to have the robot head track attendees at the Moon 2026 event.  Black star ★

## Conclusions

- MediaPipe is likely a topic of ongoing interest among the group, highlighting its importance in enhancing robotics capabilities.
