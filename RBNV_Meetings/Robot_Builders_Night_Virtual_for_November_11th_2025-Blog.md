# Innovative Insights from Robot Builders Night Virtual – November 11, 2025

The November 11th Robot Builders Night Virtual meeting brought together robotics enthusiasts and professionals in the Dallas area to explore cutting-edge developments, share project updates, and brainstorm solutions to technical challenges. In this blog post, we delve into the most exciting topics from the meeting—from competition previews and sensor technology hurdles to community-driven project collaborations.

---

## A Glimpse Into the Future: The Robo Columbus Competition

The meeting kicked off with an energizing announcement from Pat Caron, who outlined details for the upcoming Robo Columbus competition. Participants will face the challenge of programming their robots to autonomously navigate and locate traffic cones arranged in a triangle pattern. The competition is designed to emphasize precision in sensor integration and robust navigation strategies. With plans to record and share the event on YouTube, this competition promises to be an inspiring showcase of ingenuity and technical excellence.

## Showcasing Innovation: Member Projects and Updates

### IRA: The Conversational Shop Cart Robot

Mark de la Chapelle presented his intriguing project—a shop cart robot named IRA. This robot seamlessly integrates a local Large Language Model (LLM) server with Whisper CPP and a Raspberry Pi. Aside from its conversational abilities, IRA also demonstrates object detection features, positioning it as a versatile platform for both retail and robotics research. This innovative approach to blending AI with robotics not only enhances user interactions but also opens up new avenues for interactive service robots.

## Overcoming Sensor Technology Challenges

### Time-of-Flight Sensor Limitations in Bright Sunlight

Ray Casler shared his hands-on experiences with time-of-flight (ToF) sensors, highlighting their reduced performance in bright sunlight. To mitigate these issues, Ray suggested practical solutions like using visors and polarizing filters. His insights stress the importance of sensor calibration and environmental adaptations to maintain reliable operation in varied conditions.

### LIDAR and Navigation Insights from Mike Williamson

Mike Williamson further enriched the discussion by diving into the navigation capabilities of his robots. By integrating LIDAR with time-of-flight sensors, Mike demonstrated how these complementary technologies can be leveraged for more robust spatial awareness—despite challenges such as GPS inaccuracies and LIDAR's sensitivity to inclined terrains. His data-driven approach underscores the necessity for continuous testing and refinement in sensor-based navigation systems.

## Technical Hurdles and PCB Development

### Micro Mouse PCB Challenges

Matthew Komitsky shared updates on his micro mouse PCB project, outlining the hurdles posed by ordering errors and component compatibility issues. With plans on integrating and testing motors, Matthew's experiences serve as a valuable reminder of the importance of meticulous component selection and quality control in electronics design.

## Navigating Software Visualization Tools

Kareem Ponder brought attention to the challenges faced when using Fox Glove for visualization, comparing it unfavorably to Rviz. This discussion not only highlighted the gap between different software tools in meeting user needs but also opened the floor for suggestions on improving visualization frameworks—a key component in robotics development and troubleshooting.
