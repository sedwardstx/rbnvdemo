# Innovation in Motion: Highlights from the April 14, 2026 RBNV Meeting

The Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) continues to fuel the robotics revolution with its engaging Robot Builders Night Virtual sessions. On April 14, 2026, members gathered virtually to showcase inventive robotic projects, share technical hurdles, and discuss the exciting future of community robotics. In this post, we dive into the standout moments and technical breakthroughs from the meeting.

## A Look at Upcoming Events: DPRG Roborama 2026

One of the session’s major highlights was the announcement of DPRG Roborama 2026, scheduled for May 23rd at the Dallas Maker Space. The event promises a plethora of competitions and challenges with events like Quick Trip, Line Following Advanced, Barrel Racing, Six Can, Foursquare, and Sumo.

## Project Spotlights

### Tom Crawford’s Robot Demo: Tackling Traction and Calibration Challenges

Tom Crawford took center stage with a demonstration of his latest robotic prototype. His project, which faced traction issues on slick surfaces, underwent significant system improvements:

- **Battery System Replacement:** To overcome power inconsistencies, Tom upgraded the battery system, addressing issues related to insufficient traction.
- **Servo Calibration and Custom Software:** The demonstration highlighted the challenges of calibrating servos—marred by mechanical inconsistencies—and showcased the need for custom calibration software. The integration of additional sensors was suggested to enhance control precision and adapt to the varying responses of individual servos.

Tom’s hands-on approach exemplifies the experimental spirit of DPRG members, always ready to iterate on hardware challenges to achieve smoother operation under diverse field conditions.

### Mike Williamson’s Barrel Racing Robot: Navigating Speed and Latency

Mike Williamson captured attention with his progress on a barrel-racing robot, focusing on boosting speed and refining navigation. His discussion uncovered critical issues that many robotics developers encounter:

- **Latency Issues with ROS:** Mike experienced overshooting during rapid maneuvers—a consequence of latency in the Robot Operating System (ROS) and corresponding sensor data.
- **Controller Integration:** His plan to refine controller integration aims to synchronize sensor inputs more effectively, a key step in managing high-speed dynamics and ensuring responsive navigation.

Mike’s insights underline the importance of real-time processing and precision control in dynamic robotic applications, encouraging further discussion on latency mitigation strategies in ROS-based systems.

## Collaborative Horizons: Autonomous Lawn Mower Initiatives

A particularly forward-thinking segment of the meeting was led by Paul Bouchier, who introduced the "Robo Mo Automation Project." This initiative is set to transform repurposed RoboMo units into autonomous lawn mowers through several innovative approaches:

- **ROS Integration:** The plan is to revamp existing electronics with a ROS-based system, taking advantage of the open-source flexibility and integration power of ROS.
- **RTK GPS and AI Integration:** By incorporating RTK GPS technology, the project aims for pinpoint navigation accuracy. The addition of a Raspberry Pi 5 will facilitate AI integration, offering advanced decision-making capabilities.
- **Docker-Based Simulation Environment:** Developing a Docker-based simulation will enable robust testing and iterative improvements, a critical practice in today’s agile robotics development.

The discussion also touched on the broader potential of open-source platforms, such as the Open Mower project, highlighting the value of community collaboration and shared development in driving innovation.
