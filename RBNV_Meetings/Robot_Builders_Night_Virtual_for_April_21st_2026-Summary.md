# Robot Builders Night Virtual for April 21st, 2026

## Introduction
The April 21st, 2026, virtual meeting of the Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) featured detailed presentations on various robotic projects and hardware breakdowns. The meeting commenced with standard housekeeping announcements and updates on upcoming competitions, specifically the Robo Rama on May 23rd.

## Key Discussion Points

### Upcoming Events and Competitions
- The DPRG announced the Robo Rama event scheduled for May 23rd, featuring several competitions like advanced line following, quick trip, sumo, six can, and barrel racing.
- A monthly meeting set for April 25th will demonstrate Sensory Robotics' Unitree Goto Robot Dog at the Dallas Maker Space.

### Project Presentations

#### Barrel Racing Robot
- **Presenter:** Mike Williamson
- **Key Insights:**
  - Mike discussed improvements in his robot's speed and odometry by resolving DDS infrastructure issues and enhancing LAR scan data motion compensation.
  - The robot now runs at three meters per second, with improvements seen in rotational compensation.
  - An RViz demonstration was shared illustrating real-time compensation data and adjustments.

#### Mower Disassembly and Analysis
- **Presenter:** Paul Bouchier
- **Key Insights:**
  - Paul and his friend Rich disassembled a Robo Mo 612P mower, documenting the components and functions with labeled pictures.
  - They discovered the mower's design involved minimal mechanical switches, employing Hall sensors for enhanced reliability outdoors.
  - Various observations and suppositions about sensors, collision detection, and PCB design were discussed, emphasizing the innovative design for durability and functionality.

### Technical Insights and Queries

- Paul Urbanus and others provided insights into the battery management system and other underlying electronics of the mower.
- Discussion on different types of electric motors, their configurations, and potential modulations in the mower systems.
- Exploration of potential improvements and adaptations involving RTK GPS for better navigation and efficiency.
- Conversations about IoT modules and plans for devices with cellular connectivity for robotic applications.

### Other Projects and Ideas
- Pat Caron shared progress on a robotic project involving integrating LRA data with STM controllers.
- Discussions on communications between Python and Arduino, focusing on structuring and data transfer challenges.

## Conclusions and Insights
- Significant progress in individual projects was noted, particularly Mike Williamson's barrel racing robot and the collaborative effort to understand and document the Robo Mo mower's design.
- The session provided valuable insights into robotic hardware troubleshooting, optimizing systems for accuracy, and detailed technical problem-solving.
