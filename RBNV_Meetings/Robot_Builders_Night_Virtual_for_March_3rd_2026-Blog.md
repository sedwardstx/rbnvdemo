# Robotic Innovations Unleashed: Highlights from March 3rd, 2026

The Dallas Personal Robotics Group (DPRG) held an engaging Robot Builders Night Virtual meeting on March 3rd, 2026, bringing together enthusiasts and professionals alike. This meeting was a vibrant mix of project updates, demonstrations, and spirited discussions on robotics tools, techniques, and upcoming competitions. Read on to explore the key topics and innovations shared during the session.

---

## A Glimpse into the Future: Upcoming Competitions

One of the most exciting aspects of the meeting was a discussion about the upcoming competitive events scheduled for the weekend of May 24th. Notable highlights included:

- **Advanced Barrel Racing and Sumo Robotics:** Two dynamic competitions will be taking place at the Dallas Maker Space, promising challenging courses and creative robot designs.
- **International Participation:** With remote parallel contests, the event extends an invitation to enthusiasts worldwide, fostering a truly global competitive atmosphere.

These competitions represent an excellent opportunity for participants to test their innovative designs in a competitive yet collaborative environment.

## Spotlight on Projects and Innovations

The meeting served as an ideal platform for members to showcase their projects and share technical insights. Here are some standout presentations:

### 1. ROI Robot Rebuild by Mark R.
Mark R. provided an update on the club’s ROI robot rebuild, focusing on reinventing its power supply system. The key takeaways include:

- **New Power Distribution Board:** A reimagined power distribution system that not only supports improved performance but also preserves the robot's iconic wooden and fishing line structure.
- **Balancing Innovation with Tradition:** The rebuild exemplifies how legacy designs can be enhanced with current technologies, blending old-school charm with modern power electronics.

### 2. Philip Foster's Two-Wheeled Robot
Philip Foster introduced his two-wheeled robot with an outlook on adding advanced AI capabilities:

- **Mechanical Design & AI Integration:** The project combines robust mechanical design with future plans for AI-driven image processing using a Raspberry Pi 3.
- **Challenges with ROS2:** Philip addressed issues with integrating ROS2, a vital topic as developers transition to next-generation robotics frameworks.
- **Motor Control:** The use of the Modius R4 board adds a layer of precision to the robot's movement, reflecting current trends in motor control solutions.

### 3. Tom Crawford's Outdoor Robot
Tom Crawford’s presentation brought a mix of innovation and humor to the meeting:

- **Vintage Components Meet Modern Techniques:** Utilizing vintage screwdriver motors and 3D-printed parts, Tom’s project underscores the creative reuse of classic components.
- **Optical Pattern Tracking & Gemini AI:** After facing challenges with wheel alignment, Tom explored optical pattern tracking for feedback and recounted amusing insights from using Google’s Gemini AI tool for pattern generation.

### 4. Mark Dombrowski’s Dual Time of Flight Sensor Project
Mark D. shared his work on a cost-effective scanning LiDAR system:

- **Low-Cost LiDAR with Raspberry Pi Pico:** The innovative approach uses a dual time of flight sensor setup, delivering enhanced range detection with articulation capabilities.
- **Potential Applications:** This project could see wide applications in areas ranging from autonomous navigation to environmental mapping, offering a glimpse into the future of affordable sensing technologies.

### 5. Ray Casler's Rectangular Path Robot
Ray Casler’s demonstration showcased simplicity in autonomous movement:

- **Basic yet Effective Navigation:** Using distance sensors for wall tracking, Ray’s robot navigates a rectangular path without odometry, highlighting how even minimal setups can achieve key autonomous functions.
- **Recognition for Excellence:** Notably, Ray received a "Gold Star" in acknowledgment of his effective demonstration of autonomous navigation techniques.

## Filament & 3D Printing: Maintaining Quality in Production

A practical discussion during the meeting was the management of filament quality in 3D printing:

- **Addressing Moisture Issues:** Members exchanged tips on using desiccants and heated drying techniques to prevent moisture buildup and preserve filament integrity.
- **Importance in Robotics Prototyping:** With many robotics projects relying on rapid prototyping through 3D printing, proper filament maintenance is crucial for ensuring strong, reliable prints.

## Advanced Robotics Tools and Techniques

The session also provided an open forum for discussing key tools and techniques that are reshaping the robotics landscape:

- **IMUs for Orientation:** Accurate orientation maintenance using Inertial Measurement Units (IMUs) was a recurring topic.
- **Heading Control for Precision:** Incorporating advanced heading control mechanisms allows for more precise navigation—a crucial consideration for both indoor and outdoor robotics applications.
